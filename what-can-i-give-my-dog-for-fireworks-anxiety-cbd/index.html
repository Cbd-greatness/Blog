What Can I Give My Dog for Fireworks Anxiety: CBD?

  • admin
  • September 25, 2019
  • CBD For Pets
  • 0 Comments

Whether it’s the 4th of July, New Year’s Eve, or Memorial Day, when humans light up the sky with fireworks, there are many dogs who will be trembling with fear. No loving dog owner likes to see their pet cowering in terror, but fireworks anxiety in dogs is very common. So, what can you do to help your dog when fireworks are being used? What can you give a dog for fireworks anxiety? 

If you search the internet for what can I give my dog for fireworks anxiety, you will find that some people recommend anti-anxiety drugs. Many people, though, don’t like giving their pets drugs if there is a natural alternative. That’s why so many dog lovers have started to use CBD products to calm their dog’s distress. 

If you have a dog who is petrified of fireworks, here’s all the information you need on why your pet is frightened by fireworks and what you can do about it this 4th of July.

Why Is My Dog Scared of Fireworks?

The main reason that dogs are afraid of fireworks is that fireworks make a loud, unexplainable noise, that dogs cannot escape. An unexpected firework will startle a human being. When something startles us, our body releases adrenaline, our heartbeat and pulse rate increase, and we instinctively feel defensive. Through our understanding, we can laugh the fear off because we know that fireworks won’t hurt us. A dog, however, doesn’t know what a firework is. So, as the noises continue, they become more and more afraid.

The loud noise triggers the dog’s flight or fight response, but the dog can do nothing to make the noise stop. Simply put it is normal for a dog to be afraid of loud noises such as fireworks. Your dog does not know what they are or where they are coming from. It is impossible to explain to them that there is no need to worry. So until they can become used to what triggers their anxiety they will always need care and reassurance in its presence.

The noise of a firework is very unnatural. It is very loud, and it comes without any warning. A dog’s hearing is much more sensitive than our own, so they startle much easier than we do. If your dog is nervous generally, fireworks could be terrifying for your pet.

Past Experiences

Sometimes there is a link between a dog’s fear of fireworks and a previous experience. If the dog has been maltreated, it may associate loud noises with the bad treatment. Perhaps a previous owner often yelled at the dog. This would explain why the dog gets tenses up when it hears loud noises. This is why it is never a good idea to yell at your dog for any reason. You do not want to form or intensify any negative reactions towards loud noises. Instead, rewarding your dog with positive reinforcement is always the best way to go.

Genetically Predisposition

There is also evidence that some breeds of dogs are more susceptible to fireworks anxiety than others. So, this fear could be an element of genetics as well. For instance, Golden Retrievers, Labrador Retrievers, American Foxhounds, Beagles, and Pointers are some examples of hunting dogs. Hunting dog breeds were bred and raised around loud noises like gunshots and yelling. Because of their genetic history, these dog breeds may be less likely to have fireworks anxiety. However, It is common for any breed of dog to have a general fear of fireworks or loud noises.

What Are the Symptoms of Dog Firework Anxiety?

If your pet does have fireworks anxiety, the symptoms will be plain to see. They will vary in severity depending on how nervous your pet is, but it will be obvious if your pet is frightened by fireworks. Fireworks anxiety won’t cause any long-term issues for your dog, but it will be very distressing for your pet. This is the reason why so many people ask what I can give my dog for fireworks anxiety. The main symptoms of fireworks anxiety are:

  • Seeking Comfort
  • Shaking
  • Trembling
  • Hiding
  • Salivation
  • Urination

Most dogs that are experiencing fireworks anxiety will tremble and try to hide away behind furniture or under a table. If they are very frightened, they may urinate, or run away. In some cases, dogs become destructive when they feel anxiety. They may chew on furniture, or claw at carpets.
