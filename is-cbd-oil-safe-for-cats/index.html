Is CBD Oil Safe for Cats?

  • admin
  • July 11, 2019
  • CBD For Pets
  • 0 Comments

Recent research has suggested that CBD oil has many potential medicinal uses for cats. It relieves pain, reduces anxiety, and has powerful anti-inflammatory effects.

This has led to a growing market in cat CBD products. From CBD oil for cats to CBD infused cat treats, there is no end to the variety of CBD products for cats. The question that remains on many pet owners’ minds, though, is simple. Is CBD oil safe for cats?

Marijuana has been found to be toxic if given to animals in high doses. CBD is a compound that is found in both legal industrial hemp and marijuana. So does that mean that CBD is not safe for cats?

If you have been thinking about using CBD for your cat, here are the real story and facts.

What is CBD Oil?

CBD is the common abbreviation for a compound known as cannabidiol. Cannabidiol is one of more than 113 active cannabinoids identified in the hemp plant.

Humans and most other animals have what is called an endocannabinoid system. This is a system of receptors that is stimulated by natural endocannabinoids in the body.

When stimulated, these receptors control things like pain, inflammation, mood, and appetite. When cannabinoids like cannabidiol are introduced to the body, they have the same effect. They also help to maintain the body’s natural internal balance.

Benefits of CBD oil may include:

  • Anti-inflammatory activity
  • Reducing chronic pain
  • Blocking cell growth (Cancer)
  • Preventing the growth of blood vessels that supply tumors
  • Anti-viral activity
  • Promoting strong joints & mobility
  • Reducing seizure frequency & muscle spasms
  • Reducing anxiety & stress
  • Calming aggression
  • Relieving nausea & digestive issues
  • Benefitting aging & wellness

CBD oil is a concentrated extract made from hemp plants. It is made by passing a solvent over the plant. It’s a bit like making tea from tea leaves.

Full-spectrum CBD oil contains high levels of CBD, but very low levels THC (Tetrahydrocannabinol).

THC is another cannabinoid that causes the “high” feeling. CBD, on the other hand, is safe for all pets and non-psychoactive so it does NOT cause the “high” feeling.

Hemp vs. Marijuana

Hemp and marijuana plants belong to the same family of plants (cannabis). That has led to a lot of confusion over the safety of hemp products. Even though both plants belong to the same family, the chemical makeup of the two plants is very different.

The most important difference between the two plants is the amount of THC that each contains. Medical marijuana contains around 5-20% THC. Some strains can contain as much as 30%.

Hemp, on the other hand, only contains around 0.3% THC at the most. The THC content of hemp is so low that you could not possibly consume enough to feel any psychoactive symptoms (HEMP = ZERO HIGH).

This is another reason why many countries have now legalized hemp CBD products, but not marijuana-derived products. CBD derived from hemp is not psychoactive. It is safe, not toxic and considered safe for pets.

CBD: Same Health Benefits but Without the High

The health benefits of medical marijuana have been known for centuries. So, having answered the question of; is CBD oil safe for cats, you might ask, does CBD oil have the same medicinal benefits as marijuana?

Scientists have only begun to understand why cannabis has so many therapeutic effects. One thing they have discovered is that CBD has the same beneficial effects as cannabis. In fact, it has now been discovered that CBD counteracts the psychoactive effects of THC.

When CBD interacts with the endocannabinoid system, it helps to regulate the natural balance of the body. This is known as homeostasis. Studies have shown that this effect of CBD has wide-ranging therapeutic benefits. CBD has been proven to control seizures, reduce anxiety, relieve pain, and much more. 

Research into the use of CBD in medicine is still ongoing. It is known that CBD is safe for cats, humans and other pets. The full extent of the potential uses of CBD, though, is still in the process of being uncovered.

So… Is CBD Oil Safe for My Cat?

Cats are very picky eaters and for a good reason. They are very sensitive to many things. Marijuana can be toxic to cats. CBD oil, though, is considered SAFE for cats. 

One thing to be aware of when buying CBD oil for cats is that CBD products are not currently regulated by the FDA. It is important that you research CBD products before you give them to your cat.

Hemp-derived CBD products that contain 0.3% THC or less will not make your cat high. Cannabis-derived products with higher levels of THC, though, might.

There are lots of different types of CBD products on the market. CBD oil can vary a lot in quality. Look for lab test results. Do not always go for the cheapest option, it may contain harmful additives.

CBD oil is known to be non-toxic. There are no known negative side effects.

There have never been any reports of any cats ever having overdosed on CBD oil. 

How Much CBD Should I Give My Cat?

Because CBD oil is not regulated by the FDA or veterinarian bodies, there are no official dosage guidelines.

The dose of CBD you give to your cat will depend on the amount of CBD concentration in the product and the weight of your cat

  • We generally recommend between 0.25 mg (a Regular Dose) to 0.5 mg (a Strong Dose) of CBD per every 1 lb of bodyweight.

As there is no known toxic level of CBD oil. Your cat can never overdose on CBD. However, there is no benefit to be gained from giving your cat more CBD oil than it needs. Very high qualities of CBD may make your cat drowsy.

The best CBD products for cats will come with proper dosage guidelines. Otherwise, your vet should be able to give you some guidance. If your cat is already being given other medications, you should check with your vet before giving your pet CBD oil.

Always start with the lowest recommended dose, monitor your results, and then adjust the dosage as required.
